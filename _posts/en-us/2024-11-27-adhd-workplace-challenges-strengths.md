## Key Findings

The review reveals that ADHD presents both challenges and unique strengths in the workplace. While workers with ADHD may face difficulties with attention management and organization, they often demonstrate exceptional creativity, problem-solving abilities, and hyperfocus capabilities.

### Workplace Challenges

Inattention emerged as the most commonly cited challenge, affecting tasks requiring sustained focus and organization. Workers reported difficulties with:

- Managing multiple tasks simultaneously
- Maintaining attention in noisy environments
- Following complex instructions
- Meeting deadlines
- Organizing workspaces

### Notable Strengths

The review highlighted several strengths frequently associated with ADHD:

- Exceptional creativity and innovative thinking
- Ability to hyperfocus on engaging tasks
- Strong resilience and determination
- Effective crisis management skills
- Enhanced empathy and emotional intelligence
- Natural leadership abilities

## The Role of Environment

A key finding is that workplace environment significantly influences how ADHD manifests. Workers with ADHD often thrive in:

- Fast-paced, dynamic settings
- Roles requiring creative problem-solving
- Positions with autonomy and flexibility
- Environments that embrace neurodiversity

## Supporting Success

The review identifies several factors that promote workplace success:

- Appropriate accommodations (quiet workspaces, flexible schedules)
- Supportive management and colleagues
- Use of organizational tools and technology
- Person-environment fit

## Implications

This research challenges the deficit-focused view of ADHD, suggesting that many ADHD-related traits can become workplace assets in supportive environments. The findings emphasize the importance of workplace adaptations and highlight how neurodiversity can benefit organizations.
